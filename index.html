How Much Are Credit-Card Points Really Worth?

Point.Creditcard breaks down valuations, transfer partners, devaluations, redemption options and why the same points can be worth 0.5¢ or 2.5¢ depending on how you redeem them.

Compare point-earning credit cards

Point Valuation Basics

Point value is not fixed. It depends entirely on how you redeem:

  • Statement credit: usually 0.5¢–1¢ per point.
  • Gift cards: 0.8¢–1¢ typical.
  • Travel portals: 1¢–1.5¢ depending on bonus multipliers.
  • Transfer partners: 1.5¢–2.5¢+ with good redemptions.

Many people underestimate value by only using low-value redemption options.

How Transfer Partners Change Everything

Some cards allow points to be transferred to:

  • Airline programs
  • Hotel programs
  • Specialty loyalty ecosystems

This unlocks premium cabin flights, hotel upgrades and outsized redemptions — but introduces complexity and risk of devaluation.

Why Point Programs Devalue Over Time

Devaluations happen because:

  • Programs add more members
  • Costs rise for issuers
  • Airlines adjust award charts
  • Partner contracts change

Holding points instead of using them can reduce long-term value.

Redemption Strategies for Maximum Value

To get the highest value from points:

  • Avoid gift cards (low return)
  • Use transfer partners when available
  • Target long-haul flights or premium cabins
  • Take advantage of bonus transfer promotions

With the right strategy, points can outperform cashback cards.
